---

## 🧭 Overview

This project presents a **conceptual geometric framework** for analyzing human relationships.

Instead of describing relationships in purely abstract or emotional terms, the model uses **geometric structures** (points, lines, coordinate systems, spaces) to represent:

- Individuals  
- Relationships  
- Dynamics over time  
- Stability and instability  

⚠️ This model is **analytical, not prescriptive**.  
It aims to **understand**, not to judge or predict.

---

## 🧠 Core Idea

Human relationships, despite their complexity, may exhibit **structural patterns** that can be:

- Visualized  
- Modeled  
- Analyzed  

This framework proposes a **mapping** between human states and geometric representations.

---

## 🔁 Cyclic Nature of the Model

A key feature of this model is that it is **not linear**.

Relationships and entities evolve in **cycles**, not endpoints.

Example transformation cycle:

- Line → Coordinate System (relationship)  
- Coordinate System → Containment Space  
- Containment Space → Line (reformation)  

From there, the line can:

- Form new relationships  
- Become parallel or intersect with others  
- Enter new cycles  

👉 This means:
> There is no absolute “end state” — only continuous transformation.

---

## 🧩 Core Elements

### 🔹 Point
Represents an individual in early formation (e.g., a child).  
Potential to evolve into more complex states.

---

### 🔹 Line (Stable)
Represents a relatively stable individual.  
Has direction, continuity, and relational capacity.

---

### 🔹 Irregular Line (Unstable)
Represents instability or behavioral fluctuation.  
May stabilize or become more disordered over time.

---

### 🔹 Coordinate System (Relationship)
Represents structured relationships (e.g., marriage).

- X-axis: one individual  
- Y-axis: the other  
- Z-axis: depth (trust, time, stability, health, children)

---

### 🔹 Containment Space
Represents environments of care or control:

- Family  
- Therapy  
- Legal systems  
- Society  

Function:
- Protection  
- Stabilization  
- Transition toward independence  

---

### 🔹 Influence Field (Halo)
Represents social or symbolic influence.  
Extends across time and space, affecting others.

---

### 🔹 Profile (Metadata)
Describes an individual:

- Social status  
- History  
- Stability  
- Health/legal context  

Affects:
- Relationship formation  
- Stability  
- Interaction outcomes  

---

## ⚙️ Principles

### 1. Representation
Geometric elements are **abstract mappings**, not literal equivalences.

---

### 2. Transformation
Entities evolve:

- Point → Line  
- Stable ↔ Unstable  
- Individuals → Relationships  

---

### 3. Dynamics
Stability is not fixed.  
It emerges from continuous interaction.

---

### 4. Interaction
All elements influence each other.

---

### 5. Containment
Temporary environments prevent collapse and support growth.

---

### 6. Gradual Change
Most changes occur progressively.

---

### 7. Relativity
Context defines behavior.

---

### 8. Cyclicity (Core Principle)
Entities do not reach final states.

They move through recurring cycles of:

- Formation  
- Interaction  
- Transformation  
- Reformation
